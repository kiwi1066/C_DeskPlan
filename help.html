Overview

The Desk Planner lets you visually assign desks to teams across the working week using an interactive floor plan. Navigate between buildings and floors, add teams, allocate desks by day, compare days side-by-side, and export your plan as a CSV or image.

Your work is automatically saved to the browser after every change, per building and floor — you can close and reopen the tab without losing data. Use ↓ Export ▾ to create a portable backup file.

Basic Workflow

  1. Select a Building and Floor from the dropdowns at the top of the controls panel.
  2. Choose a Day from the day dropdown.
  3. Add teams using the ➕ Add Team button, or import a previous export with 📂 Import.
  4. Select one or more desks on the floor plan.
  5. Click a team chip in the Teams legend to assign the selected desks.
  6. Repeat for each day, or use 📋 Copy Day to duplicate a day's assignments across the week.
Tip: Hover over any coloured desk to see a tooltip showing the team name.

Building & Floor Navigation

The Building and Floor dropdowns at the top of the controls panel let you switch between locations. Each floor has its own completely independent set of desk assignments and teams — switching floors saves the current floor automatically before loading the new one.

Note: Teams and desk assignments are stored per floor. A team called "Finance" on Level 7 is independent of "Finance" on Level 11. Use ↓ Export on each floor to back up each plan separately.

Selecting Desks

Three ways to build a selection:

  • Single click — selects one desk and clears any previous selection.
  • Ctrl+click (or Cmd+click on Mac) — adds or removes a desk from the selection without clearing others.
  • 🤱 Multi ON — when active, every click adds to the selection automatically, no need to hold Ctrl.

Clicking an empty area of the floor plan clears the selection.

Shift+Drag Area Select

Hold Shift and drag across the floor plan to draw a selection rectangle. All desks whose area overlaps the rectangle are added to the selection on release.

  • The cursor changes to a crosshair when Shift is held, signalling drag-select is ready.
  • Drag-select is always additive — existing selected desks are kept, so you can draw multiple regions.
  • Accidental micro-drags (under 6px) are ignored and treated as a normal click.
  • Drag-select is disabled in Compare mode.
Workflow: Use Shift+drag to select a zone roughly, then Ctrl+click to fine-tune individual desks.

Zoom & Pan

Use the zoom controls above the floor plan to get a closer look at dense areas.

  • ＋ / － buttons — zoom in or out, centred on the middle of the map.
  • Scroll wheel — zoom in or out centred on wherever your cursor is, so you can point at a desk cluster and scroll straight into it.
  • ⤢ Fit button — appears when zoomed in; returns the view to full fit.
  • Click and drag on an empty area of the floor plan — pans around when zoomed in.
Tip: Zoom in first, then use Shift+drag to make precise area selections on dense parts of the floor plan. All selection methods work normally at any zoom level.

Managing Teams

Adding teams

Click ➕ Add Team to open the team builder. You can add up to 10 teams at once, each with its own colour:

  • Each row has a coloured swatch, a name field, and a remove button.
  • Click + Add another to add more rows (max 10).
  • Press Enter on the last row to add another row, or on any other row to submit.
  • Empty rows are skipped on submit — only named rows become teams.
  • The Add button updates live to show the count, e.g. Add 3 teams.

Choosing a colour

Each row starts with the next unused palette colour assigned automatically. To change it:

  • Click the row's swatch — a popover opens showing the full colour palette.
  • Colours already in use (by other rows in this batch or by existing teams) are dimmed and disabled so every team gets a unique colour.
  • The currently-selected colour for the row is outlined in black.
  • Click any available swatch to select it. The previously-held colour returns to the available pool for other rows.
  • Click the rainbow swatch at the end of the popover to open a native colour picker for any custom hex colour — useful for matching brand colours.
Tip: The palette has 22 hand-picked colours chosen to be clearly distinguishable from each other. Beyond that, the legend will warn you that colours may start to look similar.

Renaming a team or changing its colour

Hover over any team chip in the Teams legend — a ✏️ edit button appears on the right edge. Click it to open the team editor.

  • Edit the name and press Save or Enter.
  • To change the colour, untick Auto-pick colour and click a swatch from the grid below.
  • Tick Auto-pick colour to revert to the auto-assigned palette colour.
  • Press Escape to cancel without saving.
The team's ID (e.g. T1) never changes — only the display name and colour are updated. IDs stay consistent so teams can be reliably matched on import/export.

Deleting a team

Open the team editor (✏️) and click Delete. If the team has existing desk assignments you will be offered two options:

  • Delete & Unassign — removes the team and clears all its desk colours across every day.
  • Delete, Keep Colours — removes the team from the legend but leaves desk colours in place, useful when gradually reassigning desks.

Assigning Desks to a Team

  1. Select one or more desks using any selection method above.
  2. Click the team's coloured chip label in the Teams legend.
    (Click the label area — not the ✏️ edit button.)
  3. Desk colours update instantly and the summary panel refreshes.

To unassign desks, select them and use 🧿 Clear Desks.

↩ Undo Reverts the last desk-assignment change. Supports up to 50 undo steps per session.

Compare Mode

Switch the Mode dropdown to Compare to view two days side-by-side on the same floor plan. Use the two day selectors that appear to choose which days to compare.

  • Desk editing, drag-select, and zoom are disabled in Compare mode.
  • Switch back to Single to resume editing.

Desk Allocation Summary

The panel below the Teams legend updates in real time and shows:

  • Total — total desks on the floor.
  • Assigned — desks with a team assigned for the current day.
  • Free — desks with no assignment for the current day.
  • A colour-coded count badge for each team.

Import & Export

Export options

Hover over ↓ Export ▾ to choose between two modes:

  • Export All Teams — includes every team in the legend regardless of whether they have any desks assigned. Use this when you want to carry your full team roster forward to next week so you don't have to recreate it.
  • Export Assigned Teams — includes only teams that have at least one desk assigned on any day. Use this for a clean weekly report or when sharing the plan with people who only need to see active allocations.

CSV file format

Both export modes produce the same two-section format, re-importable via 📂 Import:

#teams
#category,Teams
id,name,color
T1,Finance,#fc2003
T2,Engineering,
T3,HR,#0377fc
#data
desk,mon,tue,wed,thu,fri,mon_name,tue_name,wed_name,thu_name,fri_name
desk-001,T1,T1,T2,,T1,Finance,Finance,Engineering,,Finance
desk-002,T3,,,T3,,HR,,,HR,
  • The #teams section lists the teams included in this export. The color column holds any custom or chosen colour as a hex code; if blank, the team uses its default palette colour based on its ID.
  • The #data section has one row per desk with team IDs for each day, plus team name columns for readability in Excel.
  • Older CSV files exported before custom colours were supported still import correctly — teams will fall back to their default palette colours.
Recommended workflow: At the start of the week, use Export All Teams from last week's plan as your starting point — re-import it to get all teams back instantly, then adjust desk assignments for the new week.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Add multiple teams in one go — the team builder accepts up to 10 at a time and assigns unused colours automatically.
  • Use the rainbow swatch in the colour popover to match brand colours exactly via the custom colour picker.
  • Zoom in first when selecting desks in dense areas — scroll wheel zooms to wherever your cursor is.
  • Use Shift+drag for large zones, then Ctrl+click to fine-tune individual desks.
  • Use Copy Day to roll Monday's plan across the week, then adjust individual days.
  • Use Compare mode to review differences between two days before finalising.
  • Use Export All Teams at the end of each week — re-importing it next week restores your full team roster (including custom colours) instantly without having to recreate it.
  • Use Export Assigned Teams when sharing a plan with others — it gives a clean view of only the teams actively using the floor.
  • Each floor saves independently — remember to Export each floor you work on if you want a portable backup.
  • Use Delete, Keep Colours when retiring a team mid-week — the floor plan stays readable while you reassign desks gradually.
  • Team IDs (T1, T2…) never change, so teams stay consistent across exports, re-imports, and floor switches.
